# Medivation — Three-way comparison report

**Deal:** Pfizer / Medivation (SC TO-T, announced 2016-08-22, closed 2016-09-28)
**Filing:** `data/filings/medivation/raw.md` (1315 lines; Background section pp. 24–27 of the Offer-to-Purchase exhibit)
**Auditor:** Claude (general-purpose Opus, 2026-04-21)

## TL;DR

- **Winner on this deal: bids_try.** It is the only source that correctly encodes §B5 receipt-date directionality on the Sanofi letter, correctly cites filing pages (with the canonical §P-R2 substring discipline), correctly atomizes NDAs under §E2.b, correctly classifies the two Pfizer advisory banks plus Evercore plus Guggenheim as `IB`+`advisor_financial` rows, and correctly emits deal-level `target_legal_counsel` / `acquirer_legal_counsel` / `termination_fee: $510M`. bids_pipeline is next closest (filing citations in `comments_1` are mostly accurate but the row shape is still Alex-legacy). Alex's reference is the most defective — off by ~2 days on the Sanofi start-of-process date, uses the deprecated `"Informal" / "Formal" / null` in-`bid_note` convention, omits three of the four IB rows the rulebook requires, and emits the two known-invalid §Q4 duplicates (6066/6070 BidderID=5) that Alex himself pre-flagged.
- **Top 3 divergences worth Austin's attention:**
  1. **Sanofi's first-contact date.** Filing p. 24: *"On April 15, 2016, Dr. David Hung ... received a letter dated April 13, 2016 from the Chief Executive Officer of Sanofi, setting forth a non-binding proposal to acquire Medivation for $52.50 per share."* By §B5 this is an incoming communication → anchor on receipt date = 2016-04-15. **Alex says 2016-04-13 (letter date) — wrong.** bp and try both say 2016-04-15 — right. (All of Alex's 4 Sanofi-originated rows 6060/6061/6062/6074 carry 2016-04-13.)
  2. **NDA initiation structure on Pfizer (2016-06-29).** Filing narrates a four-event cluster that day: June 28 IB contact → June 29 NDA → July 5 press release → etc. Alex emits 4 rows but conflates J.P. Morgan's IB retention to 2016-06-29 (cell `bid_date_precise=2016-06-29`, rough `2016-05-02`) with a corresponding hand-edited comment questioning the date. bids_try correctly moves J.P. Morgan, Evercore and Centerview all to 2016-05-11 (their first narrated action — §J1 inference) and adds Guggenheim on 2016-07-09 (midpoint of the July 7–11 range, §B4). bp adds only one — Pfizer — as `Bidder Interest` on May 2, omitting every IB row. None of the three sources is strictly right — see §5 "Specific rule/prompt fixes" below.
  3. **The Alex-flagged §Q4 duplicates (rows 6066/6070, both BidderID=5).** Filing p. 26 establishes July 19 as the `Final Round Inf Ann` date (process letter sent that day), August 8 as `Final Round Inf` submission, and August 14 as the `Final Round Ann` for a second round. Alex's xlsx puts `Final Round Inf Ann` at `bid_date_precise=2016-08-14 rough=2016-07-19` and `Final Round Ann` at `bid_date_precise=2016-08-14 rough=2016-08-10`. Both are wrong on the precise date (rough was right). bids_try places each precisely: `Final Round Inf Ann` 2016-07-19, `Final Round Ann` 2016-08-10. bp matches bids_try.
- **Alex-flagged confirmations.** Rows 6066 and 6070 (Alex-flagged) are confirmed invalid against the filing: precise dates are wrong, and the reference JSON has silently renumbered the duplicates via §Q4. The §Q5 atomization of "Several parties" (xlsx 6065/6075) is executed in `reference/alex/medivation.json` but not in the xlsx slice given as `medivation_alex.csv`; bids_try and bp both independently atomize these, with bids_try using §E5 placeholders (`Strategic 1`, `Strategic 2`) while bp simply names the one known signer (Sanofi) and emits a single additional NDA row without placeholders.

## Systemic findings

### Atomization (NDAs / "several parties" / IB rows)

- **NDAs.** bids_try is the only source that atomizes NDAs per §E2.b + §E5 correctly: one row per identifiable signer, plus §E5 placeholders for the unnamed balance (`Strategic 1`, `Strategic 2`). Alex's xlsx aggregates ("Several parties, including Sanofi"); the reference-JSON converter (`build_reference.py`) atomizes via §Q5 to match the rulebook. bp emits only one unnamed NDA signer (the named Sanofi), silently dropping the "several" count information.
- **IBs.** bids_try atomizes per §J1 (4 rows: Centerview, J.P. Morgan, Evercore, Guggenheim). Alex has 1 (J.P. Morgan only). bp has 0. This is a systematic omission on Alex's and bp's part; it violates §J1's mandatory "one IB row per named advisor" rule.
- **Rounds.** bp arguably over-atomizes by emitting separate `Final Round Ext Ann` / `Final Round Ext` rows for the 8/19 afternoon best-and-final request. §K1's `Final Round Ext` code is for formal deadline extensions; the 8/19 request here is more naturally a within-round acceleration than an extension. bids_try collapses to the cleaner `Final Round` on 8/19. Judgment call.

### Date precision

- **Alex's precise dates on the §Q4 rows are systematically wrong**, carrying the 8/14 "end-of-round" anchor for three different events that really occurred on 7/19, 8/10, and 8/19. Alex's own rough dates on those rows were correct (`7/19`, `8/10`, `8/08`, `8/14`), suggesting a manual data-entry slip. The AI sources both fix these.
- **§B5 receipt-date directionality.** Alex is wrong on Sanofi's 4/13 vs 4/15 — a canonical §B5 receipt-date violation on an incoming letter. Both AI sources right.
- **§B4 range-midpoint for Guggenheim.** Only bids_try handles the "July 7 through July 11, 2016" range per §B4 (midpoint = 7/9, flag `date_range_collapsed`). Alex and bp don't emit Guggenheim at all, so the question doesn't arise for them.

1. **§F2 / prompts/extract.md — Pfizer `public` inference.** bids_try's `public:null` for Pfizer is a defect, because Pfizer's publicly-traded status is textually obvious: the filing refers to Pfizer as "the Chief Executive Officer of Pfizer," references "the Pfizer Board of Directors" (p. 24), and the entire acquirer-side narrative is framed around a public-company acquirer.
