### Adjacent systems requiring change

1. **`bidder_type`**: drop `"mixed"`. Empirical usage: 0 rows across 9 deals × 435 events. The "mixed" semantics is recoverable from joint-bidder atomization (multiple constituent rows can be inspected). **Change:** vocabulary becomes `{"s", "f", null}`.
2. **`process_phase`**: keep, but the 180-day rule has only fired in penford and zep — confirm boundary detection is robust with concrete unit tests rather than relying on AI judgment.
3. **`role`**: keep as is. Three values cleanly distinguish bidder, advisor_financial, advisor_legal.
4. **`joint_bidder_members`**: drop the field entirely. Zero rows populate it under universal atomization (§E1/§E2.b). Schema simplification with no semantic loss.
5. **CA types (NDA / ConsortiumCA / Type C skip)**: keep all 3, but tighten ConsortiumCA classification per §I3 — current AI emits 3 of 5 ConsortiumCA rows misused (petsmart-inc rows 41, 42, 45). The vocabulary itself is fine; the prompt guidance and §I3 disambiguation table need a sharper rule on "permission to work together" (which is *not* a CA event).

### Top 5 highest-impact changes

Ranked by reduction in AI confusion based on adjudication evidence:

1. **Collapse the 6 Drop subcodes into 1 `Drop` + `drop_initiator` + `drop_reason_class`.**
   - Adjudication evidence: 12+ verdicts across deals on Drop subcode disagreements (penford 2007/2009 DropAtInf vs Drop; stec Company E/F DropAtInf vs DropTarget; providence-worcester DropTarget vs DropBelowInf; mac-gray DropTarget vs DropBelowM; saks Sponsor A/E DropBelowM vs DropTarget; providence-worcester Party B "needs-Austin" on DropBelowM vs DropTarget).
   - The subcodes encode 2 orthogonal axes (initiator, reason) that *are* knowable from filing language but are mashed into a 6-way string. Splitting them into structured columns lets the AI answer 2 simpler questions (is this a target or bidder action? what stated reason?), and lets `drop_reason_note` carry the free-text nuance.

2. **Collapse the 9-code Final Round matrix into `Final Round` + booleans.**
   - Adjudication evidence: imprivata both-wrong on `Final Round Ext Ann` vs `Final Round Ann` (2026-04-27); saks both-wrong on `Final Round` date; petsmart-inc missed `Final Round` and `Final Round Ext`; zep alex-right on `Final Round Inf` structural row; providence-worcester alex-right on missing `Final Round`. At least 7 adjudication entries trace to confusion over the 9-cell matrix.
   - The matrix encodes a structural 2×2×2 cross (announcement vs submission × informal vs formal × extension vs not). Booleans on a single `Final Round` event eliminate the cross-product code soup, and the `informal` axis is already encoded on paired Bid rows via `bid_type`.

3. **Drop `bidder_type = "mixed"`.**
   - Empirical evidence: 0 rows across 9 deals use `"mixed"`. Universal atomization (§E1/§E2.b emits one row per identifiable consortium constituent) means each constituent row carries its own scalar `"s"` or `"f"`. "Mixed" is a derived deal-level property, not a row-level type.
   - Side effect: §F1's decision rule simplifies (no row 5 for "consortium").

4. **Merge `Target Interest` into `Bidder Interest` with a note distinguishing target-initiated.**
   - Empirical evidence: 3 emissions across 9 deals (33% misuse rate at stec). Boundary requires AI to judge "did the target make first contact, or did the bidder?" — a fact filings sometimes muddy. Folding into `Bidder Interest` eliminates the misuse.
   - The semantic distinction is recoverable from `additional_note` and `source_quote` for downstream research.

5. **Tighten ConsortiumCA + add a CA-misuse trap.**
   - Empirical evidence: petsmart-inc emits 5 ConsortiumCA rows; 3 are filing-language misuses (`alex-right` per adjudication). The §I3 disambiguation table needs a hard rule: *"Permission to work together is not a CA event. A CA event requires the filing to say 'entered into / executed a confidentiality agreement.'"*

### Start-of-process (6 codes)

#### 1. `Bidder Interest`

- **AI usage:** 31 rows, 8 deals (every deal except medivation lacks a name-shift, but medivation has 2). All 9 deals contain at least one matching event in the filing.
- **Filing-grounded frequency:** ~9/9 deals contain at least one `Bidder Interest` event. Real, common.
- **Filing language signature:**
  - imprivata p.28: *"In early 2015, and again in June 2015, representatives of Thoma Bravo informally approached and had brief meetings with representatives of the Company, and expressed Thoma Bravo's potential interest in a possible transaction"*
  - penford p.32: *"Party A's CEO informally discussed Party A's potential interest in acquiring or combining with Penford"*
  - stec p.32: *"Company A's banker requested a preliminary meeting for a possible transaction"*
  - Common phrasing: "informally approached", "expressed interest", "discussed potential", "no specific proposal".
- **Boundary clarity:**
  - vs `Bidder Sale` — boundary is "concrete price stated OR explicit intent to acquire at $X". Imprivata Thoma Bravo cleanly transitions Interest → Sale on March 9, 2016 when the filing shifts from "expressed potential interest" to "indication of interest letter at $15.00 per share." Boundary is mostly clean in language.
  - vs `Target Interest` — boundary is "who made first contact?" Filings don't always make this explicit. The stec misuse confirms the boundary is fuzzy.
  - **Risk:** providence-worcester adjudication found AI over-emits `Bidder Interest` for already-active bidders' later contacts (continuation events). The code is being used as a catch-all for "exploratory bidder contact" rather than strictly for first-contact initiation.
- **Confusion evidence:**
  - providence-worcester `both-wrong`: AI emits `Bidder Interest` for August 9 G&W and August 12 Party B, which are continuation events, not new initiations.
  - penford `both-wrong`: AI emits a July 8 Ingredion `Bidder Interest` for a voicemail with "no purpose stated" — should not have been a row at all.
- **Verdict: KEEP.** Filing-language signature is concrete, used productively in 8/9 deals. Tighten the rule that already-active bidders' later diligence/negotiation contacts do *not* generate fresh `Bidder Interest` rows.

#### 2. `Bidder Sale`

- **AI usage:** 7 rows across 4 deals (medivation 1, penford 4, providence-worcester 1, imprivata 1).
- **Filing-grounded frequency:** 4–5 of 9 deals have at least one such event (penford has 4 stale-prior + Ingredion + Party A; medivation has Pfizer's May 20 reiterating intent; imprivata Thoma Bravo March 9; providence-worcester Party C; saks has Sponsor A and Hudson's Bay early concrete-price indications that under §C4 became `Bid` rows with `bid_type = informal` and `pre_nda_informal_bid` flag, not `Bidder Sale`).
- **Filing language signature:**
  - imprivata p.29: *"Thoma Bravo would be interested in acquiring the Company for cash at a purchase price of $15.00 per share"*
  - penford p.30: *"Mrs. Gordon advised Mr. Malkoski of Ingredion's interest in acquiring Penford"*
  - medivation p.25: *"reiterated Pfizer's interest in pursuing a negotiated transaction with Medivation when and if Medivation … decided to pursue one"*
  - Common phrasing: "interest in acquiring", "intent to make an offer", "would be willing to acquire".
- **Boundary clarity:**
  - vs `Bidder Interest` — clean in principle (concrete price OR explicit acquisition intent), murky in some borderline cases (medivation Pfizer May 20 "reiterating intent" — could be either).
  - vs `Bid` (under §D1.a unsolicited-first-contact and §C4 pre-NDA informal bid): the rulebook explicitly recasts price-bearing first contacts as `Bid` rows, NOT `Bidder Sale`. This is correct, but it means `Bidder Sale` is now a narrow code: bidder-initiated acquisition intent *without* a concrete price or terms.
- **Confusion evidence:**
  - medivation `ai-right` on Pfizer's `Bidder Sale` (5/20), but the line between this and `Bidder Interest` (his prior 5/02 row) is razor-thin and rests on the AI distinguishing "expressed interest" vs "reiterated interest in pursuing a negotiated transaction".
  - saks adjudication shows the rulebook correctly directs early-2013 Hudson's Bay/Sponsor A "interest" to `Bid` rows under §C4, not `Bidder Sale`.
- **Verdict: KEEP.** The signature is real (interest-in-acquiring without price), distinct from `Bidder Interest` (general interest), and distinct from `Bid` (concrete price). Used in 4 deals; rare but earned.

#### 3. `Target Interest`

- **AI usage:** 3 rows across 3 deals (mac-gray, petsmart-inc, stec).
- **Filing-grounded frequency:** 2 of 9 deals have a real `Target Interest` event (mac-gray Party A April 8; petsmart-inc Industry Participant March 2014). The stec emission is a misuse.
- **Filing language signature:**
  - mac-gray p.33: *"BofA Merrill Lynch, as instructed by the Board, telephoned a representative of Party A to discuss generally a possible business combination"*
  - petsmart p.28: *"the board authorized management to contact Industry Participant about exploratory merger/acquisition discussions"*
  - Pattern: target-initiated contact with a specific non-advisor party, no committed sale. **Indistinguishable** from `Bidder Interest` from a structural standpoint — same exploratory contact, same lack of price; only the direction differs.
- **Boundary clarity:**
  - **Conceptually distinct but practically near-impossible to distinguish from `Bidder Interest` based on filing language alone.** Filings often write "the Company contacted X" or "BofA reached out to X" — but reaching out to a long list of bidders during an auction is exactly what BofA does on the target's behalf, and §J1 already covers advisor outreach.
  - The specific niche this code is supposed to fill (Mac Gray's idiosyncratic Alex distinction) is narrow: target-initiated, pre-process, non-advisor-mediated, single counterparty.
- **Confusion evidence:**
  - stec `alex-right` on Target Interest misuse: the AI emitted `Target Interest` for BofA's April 1 outreach to a bidder list. That is advisor outreach, *not* target-counterparty discussion. **33% misuse rate of an already-rare code.**
- **Verdict: MERGE INTO `Bidder Interest`.** The legitimate cases (mac-gray Party A; petsmart Industry Participant) are structurally the same event — exploratory contact between target and a specific party with no price. Whether the target or bidder dialed first is preserved in `additional_note` ("target-initiated") and visible in `source_quote`. Eliminating `Target Interest` removes a 33%-misuse-rate code that requires the AI to make a directional judgment filings often muddy.

#### 4. `Target Sale`

- **AI usage:** 10 rows across 7 deals.
- **Filing-grounded frequency:** ~9/9 deals have at least one board-resolution-to-explore-sale event.
- **Filing language signature:**
  - mac-gray p.36-37: *"the Special Committee concluded that it was in stockholders' best interests 'to consider a potential sale of Mac-Gray'"*
  - petsmart p.29: *"the board determined to explore strategic alternatives, including a possible sale, and to commence a process to determine sale value"*
  - imprivata p.31: *"the Board determined it was in the best interests of the Company and stockholders to take steps to further explore a potential business combination"*
  - Pattern: board-level resolution / authorization for sale process.
- **Boundary clarity:**
  - Generally clean. Filing language is explicit board action ("determined", "authorized", "approved", "concluded").
  - One zep gotcha: AI over-emitted two `Target Sale` rows (Jul 1 2013, Feb 27 2014) for the same process. Adjudication: `both-wrong`. The code can fire on every board discussion of "strategic alternatives" if the AI is loose; needs to be reserved for *operative* sale-process authorization.
- **Confusion evidence:**
  - zep `both-wrong`: AI over-emits `Target Sale`. The fix is prompt tightening, not vocabulary change.
  - imprivata `ai-right`, multiple deals where AI adds `Target Sale` Alex missed (penford August 28; saks June 5; mac-gray June 24; petsmart August 13).
- **Verdict: KEEP.** Real event, distinct filing signature (board action), broad usage. Tighten prompt to emit at most one per phase unless the filing narrates a re-authorization.

#### 5. `Target Sale Public`

- **AI usage:** 1 row across 1 deal (petsmart-inc Aug 19).
- **Filing-grounded frequency:** 2-3 of 9 deals have a "public announcement of sale exploration" event (petsmart definitively; saks's October 2013 proxy is itself the announcement, no separate row needed; medivation/stec etc. have no separate public announcement pre-deal).
- **Filing language signature:**
  - petsmart p.30: *"the Company issued a press release announcing that it determined to explore strategic alternatives for the Company to maximize value for stockholders, including a possible sale of the Company"*
- **Boundary clarity:**
  - vs `Target Sale` — the *event* is the public announcement, not the board's earlier resolution. Distinct date.
  - vs `Sale Press Release` — these two are nearly the same code. `Target Sale Public` is the announcement *event*, `Sale Press Release` is its publicity artifact. The pair generates duplicative rows for what is one filing-narrated act.
- **Confusion evidence:**
  - petsmart adjudication: AI emitted `Target Sale Public` but missed the paired `Sale Press Release` per Alex. Both conceptually refer to the same August 19 event.
  - **Sale Press Release was never fired** in production AI output — it's a vestigial code.
- **Verdict: KEEP `Target Sale Public`** (it's the event), **MERGE `Sale Press Release` into `Press Release`** with `press_release_subject = "sale"`. When the public announcement and the press release are the same act, emit `Target Sale Public` only and fold the press-release evidence into it. When the press release is a separate post-execution announcement, emit `Press Release` with the appropriate subject.

#### 6. `Activist Sale`

- **AI usage:** 4 rows across 3 deals (petsmart 2, penford 1, stec 1).
- **Filing-grounded frequency:** 4 of 9 deals have at least one activist event (petsmart JANA + Longview; penford SEACOR; stec Balch Hill; the others — medivation, imprivata, zep, providence-worcester, mac-gray, saks — have no activist).
- **Filing language signature:**
  - petsmart p.29: *"JANA Partners filed a Schedule 13D advocating strategic alternatives including a sale; Longview's July 7 public letter urged the board to consider a sale"*
  - penford p.30: *"SEACOR filed a Schedule 13D amendment stating its intent to nominate four directors"*
  - stec p.33: *"Balch Hill filed an amended 13D and issued a board letter urging cost reductions, product focus, and exploration of strategic alternatives"*
  - Common phrasing: "Schedule 13D", "open letter", "urged", "nominate directors", "campaign".
- **Boundary clarity:**
  - vs `Bidder Interest` / `Bidder Sale` — clean. Activists pressure for *change* without proposing to *acquire*.
  - vs `Target Sale` — clean. Activists are external; `Target Sale` is the target's response.
- **Confusion evidence:** None. Adjudications affirm AI-right on every activist row. Petsmart's `both-wrong` is on JANA + Longview both being activists (Alex collapsed; AI atomized) — § D1.b's multi-activist atomization rule is correct.
- **Verdict: KEEP.** Distinct filing signature, used in 4 deals, no boundary confusion.

#### 12. `ConsortiumCA`

- **AI usage:** 5 rows in 1 deal (petsmart-inc).

##### 13a. Drop subcode consolidation proposal

The 5 narrated Drop subcodes (`Drop`, `DropBelowM`, `DropBelowInf`, `DropAtInf`, `DropTarget`) plus `DropSilent` encode 2 orthogonal axes:

| Axis | Values | What it captures |
|---|---|---|
| Initiator | `bidder` / `target` / `unknown` | Who decided to end the engagement |
| Reason class | `below_market` / `below_minimum` / `target_other` / `no_response` / `never_advanced` / null | Stated reason from filing |

The 5 subcodes are a 2D matrix encoded as a 5-element string:

| Current code | drop_initiator | drop_reason_class |
|---|---|---|
| `Drop` | `unknown` | null |
| `DropAtInf` | `bidder` | `no_response` (typically) |
| `DropBelowInf` | `target` | `never_advanced` |
| `DropBelowM` | `target` | `below_minimum` |
| `DropTarget` | `target` | `target_other` |

**Why merge:**
1. **Misuse evidence is concentrated on the initiator/reason axes, not on the existence of a drop event.** Adjudications repeatedly flag confusions like "should be `DropTarget` not `DropAtInf`" (stec Company E/F) or "should be `DropBelowM` not `DropTarget`" (mac-gray Party A) — these are reason-class disputes, not "is it a drop?" disputes.
2. **The matrix has cells the AI cannot reliably distinguish.** The boundary between `DropTarget` (target rejects for "other reasons") and `DropBelowInf` (target doesn't advance) is judgment-heavy. Stec Company E/F was flagged `alex-right` because "limited assets" reason should be `DropTarget`, not `DropAtInf`. But under the merged proposal, the AI has to identify TWO simpler facts: (a) target or bidder ended this? (b) what did the filing say the reason was?
3. **Validator §P-D5 already operates on the prefix "Drop" of any code.** Merging removes the need for a string-prefix predicate; the validator checks `bid_note == "Drop"`, then can route by `drop_initiator` for finer-grained checks if ever needed.

**Lost dimension recovery:**
- Statistical analysis of "voluntary vs target-cut" exits → group by `drop_initiator`.
- Subgroup of "below stated minimum" cases (rare) → `drop_reason_class == "below_minimum"`.
- Subgroup of "never advanced" cases → `drop_reason_class == "never_advanced"`.

**Validator implications:**
- §P-D5 unchanged (engagement requirement).
- New soft check `drop_initiator_inconsistent`: if `drop_initiator == "target"` but `drop_reason_class == "no_response"`, flag (these don't co-occur).
- `drop_reason_note` (free text, already present) continues to capture the bidder's stated reason.

#### 14. `DropBelowM`

- **AI usage:** 4 rows across 3 deals (mac-gray, providence-worcester, saks).
- **Filing-grounded frequency:** ~3-4 of 9 deals have a "target rejects below minimum" event.
- **Filing language signature:**
  - mac-gray p.42-43: *"the Special Committee chose CSC/Pamplona over the other indications and authorized moving toward exclusivity only if CSC/Pamplona increased above $20.75 to $21.25"* — implies Party A and Party B's prior indications were below the threshold.
  - saks p.34: *"the board would not agree to an acquisition below $16/share; Sponsor A and Sponsor E did not indicate readiness to improve beyond the $14.50-$15.50 range"*
  - providence-worcester p.39: Party B asked to increase, would not, Board selected G&W.
- **Boundary clarity:**
  - vs `DropTarget` — both are target-initiated. Boundary is "did the target state a minimum below which they would reject?" The minimum is sometimes implicit (saks $16) and sometimes explicit (providence Board specifying superior offer). Adjudications show this boundary is fuzzy: providence-worcester Party B is `needs-Austin` precisely because of this fuzziness.
  - vs `DropBelowInf` — one is "below stated minimum at any stage", the other is "did not advance past informal."
- **Confusion evidence:**
  - providence-worcester `needs-Austin` on Party B `DropBelowM` vs `DropTarget`.
  - saks AI-right on Sponsor A/E `DropBelowM` (filing language: "would not agree below $16").
  - mac-gray AI-right on Party A `DropBelowM`.
- **Verdict: MERGE INTO `Drop`** with `drop_initiator = "target"`, `drop_reason_class = "below_minimum"`. The filing language signature ("would not agree below $X", "did not increase to match superior offer") becomes a guideline for `drop_reason_class`, not a vocabulary entry.

### Round structure (9 codes — Final Round matrix)

The 9 Final Round codes encode a 2×2×2 cross of three orthogonal binary dimensions:

| Code | Announcement (Ann) | Extension (Ext) | Informal (Inf) |
|---|---|---|---|
| `Final Round Ann` | Y | N | N |
| `Final Round` | N | N | N |
| `Final Round Inf Ann` | Y | N | Y |
| `Final Round Inf` | N | N | Y |
| `Final Round Ext Ann` | Y | Y | N |
| `Final Round Ext` | N | Y | N |
| `Final Round Inf Ext Ann` | Y | Y | Y |
| `Final Round Inf Ext` | N | Y | Y |
| `Auction Closed` | — | — | — (separate semantics: target halts without announced deadline) |

Plus `Auction Closed` for the no-deadline case.

#### 20-28. Final Round* (9 codes)

- **AI usage (consolidated):**
  - `Final Round Ann`: 7 rows / 7 deals
  - `Final Round`: 2 rows / 2 deals
  - `Final Round Inf Ann`: 3 rows / 3 deals
  - `Final Round Inf`: 1 row / 1 deal
  - `Final Round Ext Ann`: 3 rows / 2 deals
  - `Final Round Ext`: 1 row / 1 deal
  - `Final Round Inf Ext Ann`: **0 rows**
  - `Final Round Inf Ext`: **0 rows**
- **Filing-grounded frequency:** 6-7 of 9 deals have at least one final-round event. The 8 surviving codes (excl. `Auction Closed`) are unevenly used; 2 never fire; 4 fire in 1-3 deals only.
- **Filing language signature:**
  - imprivata p.35: *"Barclays sent final bid process letters to Sponsor B and Thoma Bravo requesting marked merger-agreement drafts by July 7 and final bids by July 8"* — `Final Round Ann`.
  - stec p.38-39: *"the board directed BofA Merrill Lynch to request best-and-final proposals by May 30"* — `Final Round Ext Ann`.
  - mac-gray p.41: *"BofA Merrill Lynch sent letters on August 27, 2013 requesting revised written proposals by September 9"* — `Final Round Inf Ann` (request was for non-binding).
- **Boundary clarity:**
  - The 3 axes (Ann, Ext, Inf) are independently observable in filings.
  - The 9-code matrix is hostile: AI must compose 3 binary decisions into one string lookup.
  - Adjudications repeatedly show AI confusing announcement vs submission, and extension vs first-final.
- **Confusion evidence:**
  - imprivata `both-wrong`: AI emitted `Final Round Ext Ann` 6/24, should be `Final Round Ann` 6/24 (not extension).
  - imprivata `both-wrong`: AI emitted `Final Round Ann` 6/12 (advance to second phase), should be `Final Round Ann` 6/24 (process letter date).
  - saks `both-wrong`: AI emitted `Final Round Ann` 7/2 but missed `Final Round` 7/11; Alex emitted `Final Round` on 7/2 (wrong date).
  - petsmart `both-wrong`: AI emitted 2 `Final Round Ext Ann` rows; missed paired `Final Round` and `Final Round Ext` submission events.
  - zep alex-right: AI missed `Final Round Inf` 4/14 structural row.
  - providence-worcester alex-right: AI missed paired `Final Round` 8/12.
- **Verdict: RESTRUCTURE AS MODIFIERS.** Collapse 8 codes (excl. `Auction Closed`) into:
  - **One vocabulary code: `Final Round`**
  - **Three boolean modifiers (event-row columns):**
    - `final_round_announcement: bool` (true if filing announces / requests, false if submission/deadline)
    - `final_round_extension: bool` (true if this is an extension or a re-request after a first final-round)
    - **Informal-ness is NOT a separate modifier**: the `Final Round` event row inherits informal-ness from whether the *paired Bid rows* (on the same date or adjacent) carry `bid_type = "informal"`. If the AI emits a `Final Round` row paired with informal Bid rows, the round is informal. Adding `final_round_informal: bool` is redundant with bid_type evidence on the actual bids; in the §K2 inferred case, mark `bid_type = "informal"` on the related bids and the round inherits.

  **Net code reduction:** 8 → 1 = −7 codes.

  **Why this works:**
  1. The 3 axes are genuinely orthogonal. Encoding them as a flat string (`Final Round Inf Ext Ann`) is a category mistake.
  2. AI confusion in the 9-cell matrix is empirical and recurring (imprivata both-wrong on Ext Ann, saks both-wrong on Final Round date).
  3. The `informal` axis is double-counted: the bid rows already carry `bid_type` per §G1/§G2/§P-G2.
  4. Validator checks become simpler: `Final Round Ann` was a hint; under the new design, the boolean directly answers whether this is the announcement event.

  **Lost-dimension recovery:**
  - Subset analysis of "deals with final-round extensions" → `groupby(deal).filter(any final_round_extension)`.
  - Subset analysis of "informal final rounds" → query Bid rows on or near `Final Round` rows for `bid_type = "informal"`.

#### 29. `Auction Closed`

- **AI usage:** 1 row in 1 deal (zep go-shop expiry).
- **Filing-grounded frequency:** 1 of 9 deals (zep). The petsmart "go-shop closure" pattern *could* fire but is folded into `Executed`.
- **Filing language signature:**
  - zep p.42: *"during the go-shop process, representatives of BofA Merrill Lynch contacted a total of fifty-eight parties … none of the parties contacted during the go-shop process had submitted a competing acquisition proposal"*
  - Pattern: target halts/concludes without an announced final-round deadline; effectively the auction closed because no further engagement.
- **Boundary clarity:**
  - vs `Final Round` — `Final Round` has a stated deadline; `Auction Closed` doesn't.
  - vs `Terminated` — `Terminated` is a formal abandonment ending the phase; `Auction Closed` typically follows execution (post-execution go-shop expiry).
- **Confusion evidence:**
  - zep `needs-Austin`: the rule definition ("target halt without announced deadline") doesn't cleanly fit a post-execution go-shop expiry at an announced contractual deadline. The semantic boundary needs Austin's call.
- **Verdict: KEEP.** Distinct from `Final Round`, `Terminated`, and `Restarted`. Used in 1 deal but the filing-language signature is real. **Pending Austin's resolution of the go-shop-expiry edge case.** If Austin defines `Auction Closed` strictly as the original "no announced deadline" semantics, the zep go-shop row should re-classify as something else (probably nothing — fold into `Executed`'s `additional_note`).

## Cross-cutting issues

### Codes that are conceptually distinct but practically indistinguishable in filing language

1. **`Bidder Interest` vs `Target Interest`** — directionality of first contact is sometimes implicit in filings; AI misuse rate 33% (1 of 3). MERGE.
2. **`DropAtInf` vs `DropTarget`** — both at informal stage; one is bidder-initiated, the other target-initiated; filing language often blurs. MERGE into structured columns.
3. **`Bid Press Release` vs `Sale Press Release`** — same event class, just different subjects; the binary distinction belongs in a column. MERGE into `Press Release` + subject.
4. **`Final Round Ann` vs `Final Round Inf Ann`** — only differ by informal-ness, which is double-counted on the paired Bid rows' `bid_type`. RESTRUCTURE.

### Codes whose definition has drifted from intent

1. **`Bidder Interest`** — provided the filing supports any "informal contact between bidder and target", AI emits the row. Originally intended for first-contact only, but providence-worcester adjudication shows AI is using it for already-active bidders' continuation events. **Tighten prompt guidance**, but don't change vocabulary.
2. **`ConsortiumCA`** — petsmart shows AI uses it for any "bidders working together" pattern, even without explicit CA execution language. The vocabulary is correct; the application drifted. **Tighten §I3 guidance**.

### Schema dimensions encoded in `bid_note` but should be separate columns

1. **Drop initiator** — currently encoded as the choice between `DropAtInf` (bidder) vs `DropBelowInf` / `DropTarget` / `DropBelowM` (target). Should be `drop_initiator`.
2. **Drop reason class** — currently encoded as the variation among `DropBelowM` / `DropBelowInf` / `DropTarget`. Should be `drop_reason_class`.
3. **Final-round announcement** — currently the `Ann` suffix. Should be `final_round_announcement: bool`.
4. **Final-round extension** — currently the `Ext` suffix. Should be `final_round_extension: bool`.
5. **Press release subject** — currently the prefix (`Bid Press Release` / `Sale Press Release`). Should be `press_release_subject: str`.

### Schema dimensions that are separate columns but redundant with `bid_note`

1. **`bid_type` informal/formal on the Final Round structural rows** — the `Inf` suffix in `Final Round Inf*` codes encodes the same info that should appear on the paired Bid rows. After the matrix collapse, the `Final Round` row stops trying to carry `bid_type` info; it's already on the bids.

### Validator invariants that depend on a distinction the taxonomy can't reliably make

- **§P-D5 (drop without prior engagement)** — currently checks `bid_note` startswith `"Drop"`. Under the proposed merge, this check is *cleaner*: `bid_note == "Drop"` (single string match) covers the 5 narrated drop subcodes; `DropSilent` is a separate code that has its own §P-S1 backstop. Net win for the validator.
- **§P-S3 (phase termination)** — checks `bid_note ∈ {Executed, Terminated, Auction Closed}`. Unchanged.
- **No invariant currently depends on the Final Round matrix internals.**
- **No invariant currently depends on the Drop-subcode internals** beyond the `Drop`-prefix string match.

## Recommendations summary

### Immediate (no Austin decision needed)

1. **Drop `Final Round Inf Ext Ann` and `Final Round Inf Ext`** — never fired; data is empty; vocabulary is bloat. Cost-zero deletion. (Subsumed by the broader matrix collapse below.)
2. **Drop `bidder_type = "mixed"`** — never fired. Reformulate §F1's rule 5 to remove the consortium-mixed case; consortium constituents already get atomized rows with their own scalar.
3. **Drop the `joint_bidder_members` field** — never populated under universal atomization. Pure schema bloat.

### Phased restructure (atomic, then iterate)

4. **Collapse Final Round matrix (9 → 1) + 2 boolean modifiers.**
   - New code: `Final Round`.
   - New booleans: `final_round_announcement`, `final_round_extension`.
   - Informal-ness derived from paired Bid rows' `bid_type`.
5. **Collapse Drop subcodes (5 → 1) + 2 structured columns.**
   - New columns: `drop_initiator ∈ {bidder, target, unknown}`, `drop_reason_class ∈ {below_market, below_minimum, target_other, no_response, never_advanced, null}`.
   - Keep `DropSilent` separately (silence is structurally distinct).
6. **Merge `Target Interest` into `Bidder Interest`.** Add prompt guidance: "When the target initiated the contact, note 'target-initiated' in `additional_note`."
7. **Merge `Bid Press Release` and `Sale Press Release` into `Press Release` + `press_release_subject`.**

### Boundary tightening (vocabulary unchanged, prompt sharpened)

8. **§I3 ConsortiumCA misuse trap.** Add to the prompt: *"`ConsortiumCA` requires the filing to say a CA was 'entered into' or 'executed' between bidders. 'Permission to work together' or 'authorized to coordinate' are NOT CA events."* Cite petsmart row 41/42 as a misuse example.
9. **`Bidder Interest` continuation guard.** Add to the prompt: *"Do not emit a fresh `Bidder Interest` row for diligence/negotiation contact with an already-active bidder. The code is for first-contact initiation."* Cite providence-worcester G&W August 9 as a misuse example.
10. **`Target Sale` over-emission guard.** Add: *"Emit at most one `Target Sale` per phase unless the filing narrates a re-authorization event."*

### Open Austin questions resurfaced

- **`Auction Closed` semantics.** The zep go-shop expiry case is `needs-Austin` per the adjudication. Either tighten the definition to "no announced deadline" (and re-classify zep as something else) or broaden to include go-shop expirations.
- **`drop_reason_class = "below_minimum"` boundary.** providence-worcester Party B is `needs-Austin`. The merged drop schema makes this question more tractable: is "would not match superior offer" the same reason class as "would not increase above stated minimum"? Probably yes (both → `below_minimum`).
